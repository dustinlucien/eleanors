The Story of Eleanor's

Alison Lucien knows her accessories.  An alumna of Norma Kamali, Steve Madden, and Saks Fifth Avenue, with ten years of fashion-industry experience behind her, she understands that the right bag or earrings can take a vintage dress from kitsch to cool. And the same goes for bikes. She rode her vintage Schwinn—which she brought all the way from her Ohio hometown—to work for years, but she couldn’t find high-quality, fashionable accessories to add style to her ride. Lost in a sea of unflattering spandex bike shorts, tired of heavy metal racks and baskets, she was inspired to start…
Eleanor’s - Stylish bicycle accessories for ladies.  Launched in April 2012, she named her new business after her and her husband Dustin’s grandmas—ladies who shared a moniker and an enthusiasm for bicycling.  Online, you’ll find unique, versatile items for fashion-savvy bikers. Helmets that are cute, not clunky. Clothes you can bike to a party in and still be the best-dressed person there.  Gear that says “chic French girl”—not “Tour de France.” Visit Eleanor’s and you’ll also likely meet the shop mascot…
Birdie. A Feist mix rescued from North Carolina her customer-service skills are unsurpassed as she’s always eager to greet visitors (especially those with treats). This Southern gal is fond of the quiet life. She dislikes house music and motorcycles, and spends her free time chewing Dustin’s socks and snoozing under Alison’s chair in the shop.
